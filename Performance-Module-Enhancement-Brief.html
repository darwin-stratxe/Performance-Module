Performance Module

Why this matters

StratXe's performance module already handles the contract side well: KPAs and KPIs cascaded from strategy, tracked through the year, then signed off and locked. The weak spot is feedback. Right now you only get reviewed if you happened to share a project with someone, so it stays patchy and it skips the people who actually saw your work.

This redesign follows how the strongest tools lay the module out, like Lattice, Culture Amp and 15Five. The system already knows your objectives, KPIs and project results, so every page starts from what it holds and asks you to add the judgement rather than retype the facts. What follows is the module page by page: what loads when you open it, and what sits on each screen.

As an employee

01Overview

The page you land on when you open Performance.

  • Your current cycle and where it is up to.
  • Your score so far, out of 5, split into delivery and behaviour.
  • What is still due, with dates.
  • Links straight into your contract, review and plan.

Rough build: about a day.  ·  Seen in: Lattice, 15Five.

02Contract

Your performance contract, the same one StratXe has.

  • Your KPAs and KPIs, cascaded from the org strategy.
  • A target, weight and frequency on each one.
  • A field to enter actuals as the cycle runs.
  • Evidence attached against each KPI.
  • The weighted Balanced Scorecard total for the contract.

Rough build: about a day to adapt.  ·  Seen in: StratXe now; goals and OKRs in Lattice.

03Self-review

Where you write up your own cycle.

  • Your KPIs, tasks and project results, already listed for you.
  • A comment box against each one.
  • Room for your overall wins, misses and what you need.
  • File attachments.

Rough build: a day or two.  ·  Seen in: Lattice, 15Five.

04Feedback

where the 360 feedback actually happens.

  • A picker for who reviews you: self, manager, peers, direct reports, plus outside clients or suppliers.
  • The question set each reviewer gets.
  • A tracker of who has replied.
  • Results, shown only once enough people answer to stay anonymous.

Pick reviewers→Request→Replies come in→Results→Into your plan

Rough build: two to three days  ·  Seen in: Lattice, Culture Amp.

05Skills

Your competency picture.

  • A spider chart of your competencies, you against your manager.
  • A table of each competency, current against target.
  • The gaps marked, which feed your development plan.
You Your manager

Rough build: about a day.  ·  Seen in: Peoplebox, Culture Amp.

06Development

Your growth plan for the cycle.

  • Your development actions in a list.
  • An owner, a due date and a done-marker on each.
  • Actions pulled in from your feedback and skill gaps.

Rough build: a day or two.  ·  Seen in: Lattice Grow, 15Five.

Manager & HR

07My team

A manager's view of their people.

  • Each of your people and where they are in the cycle.
  • Reviews and approvals waiting on you.
  • A flag on anyone overdue or at risk.

Rough build: a day or two.  ·  Seen in: StratXe's My People; Lattice manager view.

08Reports

The roll-up layer for HR and leadership.

  • Roll-ups by team, department and competency.
  • Separate views for HR, EXCO and the Board.
  • A calibration view to line managers' scores up.

Rough build: a day or two.  ·  Seen in: Betterworks, SuccessFactors.

09Setup

Admin controls, hidden from everyone else.

  • Cycle types and dates.
  • Scoring weights.
  • Anonymity limits.
  • Who can see and do what.

Rough build: a day or two.  ·  Seen in: SuccessFactors, Workday.
